Pixiedust!

pixiedust is an R package for producing complete, publication-ready tables for statistical analyses. It is designed to give the user the ability to fine tune each individual cell of a table while keeping as much of the code of the rendering language in the background as possible.

Detailed explanations of pixiedust options are provided on this website. In time, it will host examples for all of the output types supported by pixiedust, including console output, markdown, HTML, and LaTeX.

Why Pixiedust?

There is nothing in my professional career that has caused me more angst than trying to format results from statistical analyses into attractive, presentable, and readable tables that my clients can interpret. This was especially true with respect to summarizing statistical models. For most of the analyses I did, I'd eventually cobble together some hack that did what I wanted, but it inevitably failed me, or didn't provide the flexibility I needed for special circumstances. Or worse, required me to do all manner of ugly post-processing manipulations to get the look I wanted.

One day, I said to myself in frustration, "why can't we just build tables like plots are built in ggplot?" It occurred to me then that what I had been doing wrong was that I was trying to pass an object to a single function and have one function that did everything I wanted it to do. Instead, I needed an object that could be acted upon through several functions. This inspiration, pushed along by the introduction of the broom package, gave birth to pixiedust.

The name pixiedust was chosen because I liked the imagery of sprinkling a table cell with dust and magically changing it's appearance. So far, pixiedust has been a success, at least in my work. It has avoided many of the frustrations my earlier attempts have encountered; and the few shortcomings I have found have been easily remedied. I hope it works for you, too.
